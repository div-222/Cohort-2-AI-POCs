# HR Leave Policy
**Document Type:** HR Policy  
**Category:** Leave Management  
**Version:** 2.1  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Owner:** HR Department  

---

## 1. Overview

This document outlines the leave entitlements and procedures for all full-time and part-time employees at the organization. All employees are expected to familiarize themselves with this policy and adhere to the guidelines.

---

## 2. Types of Leave

### 2.1 Casual Leave (CL)

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Entitlement | 12 days per calendar year |
| Carry Forward | Not allowed |
| Encashment | Not applicable |
| Minimum Notice | 1 working day prior |
| Maximum Consecutive Days | 3 days |

**Rules:**
- Casual leave cannot be combined with other leave types for more than 3 consecutive days.
- Casual leave lapsed at year-end will not be compensated.
- New joiners are entitled to CL on a pro-rata basis from their joining month.

---

### 2.2 Sick Leave (SL)

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Entitlement | 10 days per calendar year |
| Carry Forward | Up to 5 days (max accumulation: 20 days) |
| Encashment | Not applicable |
| Medical Certificate | Required for more than 2 consecutive days |

**Rules:**
- Sick leave should be applied on the day of absence or on the first day back.
- For leaves exceeding 3 days, a medical certificate from a registered practitioner must be submitted to HR within 3 working days of returning.

---

### 2.3 Earned Leave / Privilege Leave (EL/PL)

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Entitlement | 18 days per calendar year (1.5 days per month) |
| Carry Forward | Up to 30 days |
| Encashment | Allowed (up to 15 days per year) |
| Minimum Notice | 7 working days prior |
| Minimum Leave Duration | 3 consecutive days |

**Rules:**
- Earned leave is accrued monthly at 1.5 days and credited to the employee's leave account.
- Employees must apply in advance and obtain manager approval.
- Earned leave can be clubbed with public holidays and weekends.

---

### 2.4 Maternity Leave (ML)

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Entitlement | 26 weeks (for first two children); 12 weeks (for third child onwards) |
| Paid Leave | Yes — full pay |
| Pre-natal Leave | 8 weeks before expected delivery |
| Adoption | 12 weeks for mothers adopting a child below 3 months |

**Rules:**
- Maternity leave applies to all female employees who have completed at least 80 days of service in the 12 months preceding the expected date of delivery.
- Employees must submit a medical certificate confirming pregnancy and expected due date to HR at least 4 weeks before going on leave.
- Miscarriage: 6 weeks paid leave from the date of miscarriage.

---

### 2.5 Paternity Leave

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Entitlement | 5 working days |
| Paid Leave | Yes — full pay |
| Validity | Within 30 days of the child's birth |

---

### 2.6 Bereavement Leave

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Immediate Family | 5 working days |
| Extended Family | 3 working days |
| Paid Leave | Yes — full pay |

**Immediate family includes:** Spouse, children, parents, siblings, parents-in-law.  
**Extended family includes:** Grandparents, grandchildren, aunts, uncles.

---

### 2.7 Compensatory Off (Comp-Off)

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Eligibility | Employees who work on holidays/weekends as directed by management |
| Validity | Must be availed within 30 days of working on a holiday |
| Application | Through the HR Portal within 3 days of working on the holiday |

---

### 2.8 Leave Without Pay (LWP)

- Granted at the discretion of management.
- All accrued leave must be exhausted before applying for LWP.
- Extended LWP (more than 30 days) requires approval from the HR Head.

---

## 3. Public Holidays

Employees are entitled to **10 national and regional public holidays** per year as declared in the annual holiday calendar published by HR every December.

---

## 4. Leave Application Process

### Step-by-Step Process

```
1. Log in to the HR Portal (portal.company.com)
2. Navigate to: My Leave → Apply for Leave
3. Select Leave Type → Enter Dates → Add Reason
4. Submit → Automatic notification sent to manager
5. Manager Approves / Rejects within 2 working days
6. Employee receives email confirmation
```

### Leave Approval Matrix

| Leave Type | Approver |
|-----------|---------|
| Casual Leave | Direct Manager |
| Sick Leave | Direct Manager |
| Earned Leave | Direct Manager + HR (for > 10 days) |
| Maternity / Paternity | HR Department |
| Bereavement | Direct Manager |
| LWP | HR Head |

- **Slack:** Type `/leavebalance` in the internal Slack bot

---

## 6. Leave Encashment

- Only **Earned Leave (EL)** is eligible for encashment.
- Maximum **15 days** can be encashed per calendar year.
- Encashment is processed in the **December payroll**.
- Application must be submitted through the HR Portal by **November 30**.

---

## 7. FAQs

**Q: Can I carry forward my Casual Leave?**  
A: No. Casual leave lapses at the end of the calendar year and cannot be carried forward or encashed.

**Q: How many sick leaves can I carry forward?**  
A: You can carry forward up to 5 days per year, with a maximum accumulation of 20 days.

**Q: What is the minimum notice period for Earned Leave?**  
A: You must apply at least 7 working days in advance.

**Q: How do I apply for Bereavement Leave?**  
A: Inform your manager immediately and apply through the HR portal. You may submit supporting documentation upon return.

**Q: Can I apply for leave on the same day?**  
A: Casual and Sick leave can be applied on the same day. Earned leave requires advance notice.
