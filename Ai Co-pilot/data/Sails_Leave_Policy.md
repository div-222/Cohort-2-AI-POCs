## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the complete framework for all paid and unpaid leave at Sails Software Solutions Pvt. Ltd. ("Sails," "the Company"). It specifies how leave is earned, how much is available, how to apply for it, how approvals work, and how every common and edge-case situation — probation, resignation, emergencies, holidays, weekends, maternity, and partial days — is treated.

The objective is twofold: to give employees genuine flexibility to attend to personal life and to recover from illness, while ensuring that project deadlines, client commitments, and team coverage are never compromised. Consistent with the Company's stated values, personal time off is expected to be **the exception and not the norm**, and employees are expected to plan responsibly so that no team is left short-handed.

## 3. Definitions and Glossary

- **Leave Year:** The calendar year, 01 January to 31 December.
- **Financial Year (FY):** 01 April to 31 March, relevant to tax-linked items such as LTA.
- **Earned Leave (EL):** Paid leave accrued at **1.33 days per completed month of service**, totalling **16 working days per leave year**.
- **Accrual:** The monthly addition of leave to an employee's balance (1.33 days/month).
- **Crediting:** The act of placing leave into an employee's available balance (upfront for confirmed staff; pro-rata for new joiners).
- **Loss of Pay (LOP) / Leave Without Pay (LWP):** Unpaid leave taken once the earned-leave balance is exhausted, or where leave is otherwise unpaid. The terms are used interchangeably.
- **Compensatory Off (Comp-Off):** A day of paid leave granted in lieu of an approved, client-approved-and-billed extra day worked.
- **Maternity Leave:** Time off for childbirth and care, per Section 10.
- **Half-Day Leave:** A leave unit of 0.5 day, deducted from the earned-leave balance.
- **Week-off:** A non-working weekend day (Saturday and Sunday, unless otherwise specified by project roster).
- **Public Holiday:** A Company-declared holiday from the published annual holiday calendar.
- **Sandwich Leave:** The treatment of an intervening week-off or holiday that falls between two leave days.
- **SPOC:** Single Point of Contact appointed by the Company for leave coordination.
- **HRIS:** Human Resource Information System, hosted at **https://sailssoftware.greythr.com/**.
- **LWD:** Last Working Day.
- **F&F:** Full and Final settlement.
- **A day of leave equals a day of pay.** Conversely, a day of unpaid leave results in a proportional deduction of gross salary and applicable allowances.

## 4. Leave Year, Accrual, and Crediting

- The leave year follows the **calendar year** (01 January – 31 December).
- Earned leave accrues at **1.33 days per completed month**, equalling **16 days per full year**.
- **Confirmed (existing) employees:** the full 16 days are **credited upfront** at the start of the leave year.
- **New hires (mid-year joiners):** leave is **credited pro-rata** from the date of joining, accruing 1.33 days per completed month.
- **Probationary employees:** leave **accrues monthly** and is available only as it accrues (no upfront crediting; see Section 6).

### 4.1 Pro-Rata Crediting for New Joiners

| Joining month | Completed months remaining in leave year | Pro-rata EL credited (approx.) |
|---------------|:----------------------------------------:|:------------------------------:|
| January | 12 | 16.00 |
| April | 9 | 12.00 |
| July | 6 | 8.00 |
| October | 3 | 4.00 |
| December | 1 | 1.33 |

> Pro-rata values are rounded per HRIS configuration. Fractional balances are carried at two decimal places in the system.

## 5. Earned Leave (EL)

### 5.1 Entitlement

Every eligible employee is entitled to **16 working days of paid earned leave per leave year**, accruing at **1.33 days per completed month of service**.

### 5.2 Accrual Reference

| Months of service | Earned leave accrued |
|-------------------|:--------------------:|
| 1  | 1.33 |
| 2  | 2.66 |
| 3  | 4.00 |
| 4  | 5.33 |
| 5  | 6.66 |
| 6  | 8.00 |
| 9  | 12.00 |
| 12 | 16.00 |

### 5.3 Carry-Forward

- Unused earned leave at year-end **does not carry forward** to the next leave year. The balance resets.

### 5.4 Encashment

- Unused earned leave **cannot be encashed** as a matter of entitlement.
- Management may, at its **sole discretion**, choose to encash unused leave at year-end. This is discretionary and creates no precedent or right.

### 5.5 Negative Balance

- Earned leave **cannot be taken in advance** of accrual. An employee cannot hold a negative EL balance; any leave beyond the accrued balance is treated as **Loss of Pay** (Section 7).

### 5.6 Leave and Pay Equivalence

- One day of earned leave is paid at the equivalent of one day's pay.
- Earned leave does **not** sandwich intervening weekends (see Section 12).

## 6. Leave During Probation

The standard probationary period is the **first six (6) months** of employment. It may be **extended at the Company's discretion by up to three (3) additional months** for further assessment.

### 6.1 Entitlement During Probation

- Probationary employees accrue earned leave at the **same rate of 1.33 days per completed month**.
- Leave **accumulates monthly**; unused balance from one month carries into subsequent months within the probation period.

### 6.2 Restrictions During Probation

1. **No advance leave.** Leave that has not yet accrued cannot be used. Example: only 1.33 days exist after month 1.
2. **No over-utilisation.** Leave taken cannot exceed the accrued balance at that point. Example: an employee who has completed three months cannot take more than 4.00 days.
3. **No optional holiday.** The optional festival holiday (Section 9) is **not available** during probation.
4. **LWP for excess.** If accrued leave is exhausted and more time off is needed, the employee may apply for **Leave Without Pay**, subject to managerial approval.

### 6.3 Separation During Probation

- If an employee resigns or is terminated during probation, any unused leave balance is **not eligible for payout**.
- Employer-initiated separation during probation provides **15 days** from the notice of separation for handover (see Section 16).

## 7. Loss of Pay (LOP) / Leave Without Pay

LOP applies when an employee needs leave beyond the available earned-leave balance, or where leave is otherwise unpaid. While LOP provides flexibility for genuine and unforeseen circumstances, **excessive use is discouraged and may be factored into performance reviews**.

### 7.1 Key Rules

- LOP applies **only after all earned-leave balances are exhausted**.
- LOP requests must reach the reporting manager **at least 5 working days in advance**, except in genuine emergencies.
- Approval is **subject to business requirements and adequate staffing**.
- For each LOP day, **gross salary and applicable allowances are deducted proportionally**.
- LOP **includes any intervening week-offs** but **excludes public holidays**.

### 7.2 Pay-Deduction Scenarios

| Scenario | Pay Deduction |
|----------|---------------|
| Single LOP day on a weekday | 1 day deducted |
| Consecutive LOP days spanning a weekend | All LOP days **and** the intervening Saturday & Sunday |
| LOP days before and after a public holiday | LOP days only; the public holiday is **not** counted as LOP |
| LOP spanning a holiday and a weekend | LOP days and intervening weekend deducted; public holiday **excluded** |
| LOP for an entire week including the weekend | All weekdays **and** the weekend deducted |

### 7.3 Impact

- LOP days reduce monthly gross pay proportionally and may also pro-rata-reduce other monthly allowances.
- A pattern of LOP may be reviewed during the mid-year and year-end performance cycles.

## 8. Compensatory Off (Comp-Off)

A comp-off is a paid day off granted in lieu of an approved extra day worked.

### 8.1 Eligibility

- Comp-offs are granted **only** for extra days worked where the work was **approved by the client and billed to the client**. Internal or unbilled extra work does not qualify.

### 8.2 Request Process

1. Email the comp-off request to **HR**, with the **team manager(s) in CC**.
2. Attach **proof of client approval** for the extra work day **and confirmation of billing**.
3. Submit the request **within the same calendar month** as the extra day worked.

### 8.3 Utilisation

- Approved comp-offs must be used **within the same calendar year** in which granted.
- Comp-offs **cannot be carried forward** and will **lapse** at year-end.
- Comp-offs **cannot be encashed**.

### 8.4 Exceptions

No exceptions to these guidelines will be made unless **specifically approved by senior management**.

## 10. Maternity Leave

Female employees are eligible for **up to six (6) months** of maternity time-off, availed in one of two ways:

1. **Up to six months at basic pay** for each month of time-off availed; **or**
2. **Full pay for the first three months**, then **unpaid** time off for the remainder. Full pay resumes once the employee returns to duty.

### 10.1 Confirmation Requirement

- Employees must **confirm their chosen option at least four (4) weeks before** commencing maternity leave.
- Failure to confirm in advance may, due to material business impact, result in the Company **choosing not to pay** during the maternity leave.

### 10.2 Interaction with Earned Leave

- Maternity leave is a distinct entitlement and is **not** deducted from the earned-leave balance.
- Earned leave continues to accrue per Company practice and applicable law during the leave period.

### 10.3 Statutory Floor

Where the Maternity Benefit Act, 1961 (as amended) provides a benefit more favourable than this section, the **statutory benefit applies**.

## 11. Half-Day Leave

- Employees may apply for leave in units of **0.5 day** where operational needs permit.
- A half-day is deducted as **0.5** from the earned-leave balance.
- Half-day leave is applied for on the HRIS, with the same advance-notice and manager-approval expectations as full-day leave.
- A half-day combined with stepping away for personal work may trigger the **4-hours-per-month flexibility threshold** (Section 17.4); beyond that threshold, formal leave must be applied.

## 12. Sandwich Leave (Weekend and Holiday Treatment)

The treatment of an intervening week-off or holiday depends on whether the surrounding leave is **paid** or **unpaid**.

| Surrounding leave type | Intervening weekend | Intervening public holiday |
|------------------------|:-------------------:|:--------------------------:|
| **Paid Earned Leave** | **Not** deducted (no sandwich) | Not deducted |
| **Loss of Pay (LOP)** | **Deducted** (sandwiched) | **Not** deducted |

### 12.1 Why This Matters

When planning a long absence, exhausting earned leave and crossing into LOP **across a weekend** results in that weekend also being unpaid. Plan multi-day leave so that paid earned leave covers the days adjacent to week-offs wherever possible.

### 12.2 Illustration

- **Paid:** Thursday + Friday EL, with Sat/Sun off → **2 days** deducted from EL; weekend not charged.
- **Unpaid:** Friday LOP + Monday LOP, with Sat/Sun in between → **4 days** deducted (Fri, Sat, Sun, Mon).
- **Mixed across a holiday:** LOP on the working days either side of a public holiday → **LOP days only**; the holiday is excluded.

## 13. Leave Not Offered by the Company

The Company offers earned leave, compensatory off, and maternity leave only. It does **not** provide the following as separate categories:

- Separate **sick / medical leave** (sickness is managed within the earned-leave balance, then LOP).
- Separate **casual leave**.
- **Bereavement leave**.
- **Paternity leave** (subject to applicable statute, if any).
- **Sabbatical** or study leave (study time is managed via L&D and earned leave).
- **Marriage leave**.

Needs in these areas are handled through the **earned-leave balance**, and beyond that through **Loss of Pay**, subject to managerial approval.

> Sick employees unable to log a message themselves should have a family member call the manager or HR, who will update the team.

## 14. Applying for Leave

All leave must be applied for and approved through proper channels **before** it is taken, except in genuine emergencies.

### 14.1 Where to Apply

1. Apply on the HRIS portal: **https://sailssoftware.greythr.com/**
2. Notify the team via the Microsoft Teams channel **# Employee Leave Notifications**.
3. Inform the designated **SPOC**.

### 14.2 Advance-Notice Guidelines (Planned Leave)

| Duration of leave | Minimum advance notice |
|-------------------|:----------------------:|
| 1 day | 3 days in advance |
| 2 days | 5 days in advance |
| 3–5 days (or more) | 3 weeks in advance |
| LOP (any duration) | 5 working days in advance |
| Optional holiday | 2 weeks in advance |
| Maternity (option confirmation) | 4 weeks before start |

### 14.3 Emergencies and Sickness

- In genuine emergencies, notify your manager and SPOC as early as possible, and regularise the leave on the HRIS afterward.
- If too unwell to send a message, have a family member call the manager or HR.

### 14.4 Timesheets

- Approved client timesheets must be kept current. Leave should be reflected accurately so that billing and coverage records remain correct. Approved timesheets up to the LWD are mandatory to process the F&F settlement (Section 16).

## 15. Approval Workflow and Authority Matrix

### 15.1 Standard Workflow

1. Employee informs reporting manager (verbally / Teams) **before** applying.
2. Employee applies on the HRIS within the advance-notice window.
3. Manager reviews against project coverage and approves or declines.
4. Employee posts to **# Employee Leave Notifications** and informs the SPOC.
5. HR/HRIS records the approved leave and updates the balance.

### 15.2 Authority Matrix

| Leave type / situation | Primary approver | Secondary / Notify |
|------------------------|------------------|--------------------|
| Earned leave (1–2 days) | Reporting manager | SPOC |
| Earned leave (3+ days) | Reporting manager | HR, SPOC |
| Loss of Pay | Reporting manager | HR |
| Compensatory off | HR | Team manager (CC) |
| Optional holiday | Reporting manager | HR |
| Maternity leave | HR | Reporting manager |
| Comp-off exception | Senior management | HR |
| Step-away flexibility >4 hrs/month | Reporting manager | — (apply leave) |

### 15.3 Decision Criteria

Approvals are at **managerial discretion**, based on operational requirements, project deadlines, client commitments, and adequate team coverage. Approval is not automatic even where notice requirements are met.

## 16. Leave and Separation

### 16.1 Earned Leave on Exit

- On resignation or termination, earned-leave entitlement is **computed pro-rata up to the LWD**.
- Leave taken beyond the pro-rata entitlement is **deducted from the F&F** as Loss of Pay.
- Leave balance as of the LWD is **not encashed** and **does not form part** of the F&F settlement.

### 16.2 Leave During Notice Period

- The minimum notice period is **2 months** for confirmed employees.
- **Any leave taken during the notice period automatically extends the LWD** by an equivalent amount.
- The notice period may be extended based on project requirements and manager discretion; the Company may also reduce or waive it per business needs.

### 16.3 Probation Separation

- Employer-initiated separation during probation: **15 days** for handover.
- Unused leave on probation separation is **not paid out**.

### 16.4 F&F Timing

- F&F may take up to **45 days** from the LWD.
- Approved timesheets up to the LWD are mandatory for F&F processing.

## 17. Interactions with Other Policies

### 17.1 Leave and Public Holidays

Public holidays are not charged to the leave balance. Their interaction with LOP is governed by Section 7.2 / Section 12.

### 17.2 Leave and Leave Travel Allowance (LTA)

- LTA exemption can be claimed **only when the employee is on leave** and has actually travelled within India.
- Leave records are required as **proof** to support an LTA claim.
- LTA may be claimed **twice within a four-year block period**; group/official travel does not qualify.

### 17.3 Leave and Performance Management

- The performance cycle runs **June to May**.
- Excessive LOP and irregular attendance may be factored into mid-year and year-end reviews.
- Learning Agility and engagement (including planned L&D, which may use earned leave) contribute to overall performance.

### 17.4 Leave and Workplace Flexibility

- Work starts at **10:30 AM** (8 working hours excluding lunch/breaks).
- Stepping away for personal work requires manager/project-leader approval.
- Using this step-away flexibility for **more than 4 hours in a month** requires applying for **leave**.
- Abuse of this flexibility may attract severe action.

### 17.5 Leave and Gratuity / Continuity of Service

- Gratuity eligibility requires **five years of continuous service** (Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972).
- Approved leave does not break continuity of service; **unauthorised absence** may affect continuity at the Company's discretion.

## 18. Worked Examples

> All examples assume a confirmed employee with a five-day work week (Sat/Sun off) unless stated.

### 18.1 Mid-Year Joiner Accrual
An employee joins **01 July 2026**. They have 6 months remaining and are credited pro-rata: **6 × 1.33 = 8.00 days** for the remainder of the leave year.

### 18.2 Probation Over-Utilisation Block
An employee in month 3 of probation has accrued **4.00 days**. They request 5 days off. The system allows **4 days as earned leave** and the **5th day becomes LOP** (subject to approval).

### 18.3 Paid Leave Across a Weekend (No Sandwich)
An employee takes earned leave **Thu 04 Jun + Fri 05 Jun**, with Sat/Sun off, returning Mon. Deduction: **2.0 days EL**. The weekend is **not** charged.

### 18.4 LOP Across a Weekend (Sandwiched)
An employee with zero EL takes **Fri + Mon** as LOP, with Sat/Sun in between. Deduction: **4 days' pay** (Fri, Sat, Sun, Mon).

### 18.5 LOP Around a Public Holiday
An employee with zero EL takes LOP on the working days **before and after** Republic Day (26 Jan, Mon). The holiday is **excluded**; only the LOP working days are deducted.

### 18.6 Comp-Off Lifecycle
An employee works an extra **Saturday on 14 Feb** (client-approved and billed). They email HR (manager in CC) with proof **within February**, receive a comp-off, and must **use it before 31 Dec** of the same year or it lapses.

### 18.7 Maternity — Option 1
An eligible employee takes 6 months at **basic pay** each month. EL is not charged; statutory floor applies if more favourable.

### 18.8 Maternity — Option 2
An eligible employee takes 6 months: **first 3 months at full pay**, next 3 months **unpaid**. Full pay resumes on return.

### 18.9 Leave During Notice Period
An employee resigns with a 2-month notice and takes **5 days leave** during it. The **LWD extends by 5 days** accordingly.

### 18.10 Exit With Excess Leave
By the LWD an employee has taken **2 days more** than their pro-rata entitlement. Those **2 days are deducted as LOP** from the F&F. Any remaining balance is **not encashed**.

### 18.11 Half-Day Plus Flexibility Threshold
An employee takes a **0.5-day** EL and has separately stepped away for **3.5 hours** that month. The combined step-away time approaches the **4-hour/month** threshold; further step-aways that month should be converted to leave.

## 19. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. How many paid leaves do I get in a year?**
A1. **16 working days** of earned leave per year, accruing at **1.33 days per month**.

**Q2. When are my leaves credited?**
A2. Confirmed employees are credited the full 16 days at the **start of the year**; new joiners receive them **pro-rata** from their joining date; probationers accrue **monthly**.

**Q3. Can I carry forward unused leave?**
A3. No. Unused earned leave **does not carry forward** and resets at year-end.

**Q4. Can I encash unused leave?**
A4. Not as a right. Encashment is **at management's sole discretion** and is not guaranteed.

**Q5. Can I take leave I haven't accrued yet?**
A5. No. You cannot go into a negative balance. Anything beyond your accrued balance is **Loss of Pay**.

**Q6. Do I have sick leave or casual leave?**
A6. No separate sick or casual leave exists. Sickness and personal needs are covered by **earned leave**, then **LOP**.

**Q7. What is the notice required for a 3-day leave?**
A7. **3 weeks** in advance (3–5+ day leaves require 3 weeks' notice).

**Q8. How do I report leave?**
A8. Apply on the **HRIS (greytHR)**, post on the Teams channel **# Employee Leave Notifications**, and inform the **SPOC**.

**Q9. Will my weekend be charged if I take leave on Friday and Monday?**
A9. If it's **paid earned leave**, the weekend is **not** charged (2 days deducted). If it's **LOP**, the weekend **is** charged (4 days deducted).

**Q10. Is a public holiday deducted if it falls during my LOP?**
A10. No. Public holidays are **excluded** from LOP deductions.

**Q11. Can probationers take the optional festival holiday?**
A11. No. The optional holiday is available **only after probation** is completed.

**Q12. How do I claim a compensatory off?**
A12. Email **HR (manager in CC)** with **client approval + billing proof**, within the **same month** as the extra day worked. Use the comp-off **within the calendar year**.

**Q13. How much maternity leave is available, and how is it paid?**
A13. Up to **6 months**, either at **basic pay** throughout, or **full pay for 3 months + unpaid for 3 months**. Confirm the option **4 weeks** before starting.

**Q14. What happens to my leave balance when I leave the company?**
A14. It is computed **pro-rata to your LWD**, **not encashed**, and excess leave is deducted as **LOP** from the F&F.

**Q15. Does taking leave during my notice period change my last day?**
A15. Yes. Any leave during the notice period **extends your LWD** by the same number of days.

**Q16. Can I take a half-day?**
A16. Yes, in **0.5-day** units, deducted from your earned-leave balance, with normal approval.

**Q17. I need to step out for two hours for personal work — is that leave?**
A17. Not by itself, with manager approval. But if your step-aways exceed **4 hours in a month**, you must apply for leave.

**Q18. Does leave accrue during maternity leave?**
A18. Maternity leave is separate from earned leave; accrual continues per Company practice and applicable law.

## 20. Roles and Responsibilities

- **Employee:** Plan leave responsibly; give required advance notice; apply on the HRIS; notify the Teams channel and SPOC; keep timesheets current; arrange proper handover.
- **Reporting Manager:** Review and decide leave fairly and promptly, balancing the employee's needs with project coverage and client commitments; flag patterns of misuse.
- **SPOC:** Coordinate team-level leave visibility and notifications.
- **HR:** Maintain leave records; process comp-offs, maternity options, and exceptions; ensure consistent application; manage F&F leave computation.
- **Senior Management:** Approve exceptions; exercise discretion on encashment, notice-period waivers, and severance.

---

## 21. Compliance, Misuse, and Disciplinary Action

- Leave is a facility built on trust; misuse undermines the team and the client relationship.
- **Unauthorised absence** (leave not applied for or not approved) may be treated as LOP and may attract disciplinary action.
- **Abuse of the step-away flexibility** beyond reasonable limits may attract severe action.
- **Falsifying** leave reasons, comp-off proofs, or LTA documentation is a serious offence and may lead to disciplinary measures up to and including termination.
- Repeated or excessive LOP may be reflected in **performance reviews**.
