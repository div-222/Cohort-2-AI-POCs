## 1. Purpose

This guide gives every new hire at Sails Software Solutions a clear, structured path through their first days and months — from pre-start setup to confirmation. It consolidates the practical and policy essentials (accounts, equipment, ID cards, dress code, work hours, conduct, payroll, leave, benefits, and performance) so new joiners can become productive quickly while understanding the standards and culture of Sails.

---

## 2. Scope and Applicability

This guide applies to all new employees — full-time, probationary, part-time, contractual, and interns — and to the managers, buddies, IT, and HR staff who support them. Category-specific differences (for example, the optional holiday or L&D eligibility) are noted where relevant.

---

## 3. Definitions and Glossary

- **Onboarding:** The process of integrating a new hire into the Company.
- **Probation:** The first 6 months of evaluation (extendable up to 9).
- **Buddy:** A peer (not the manager) who helps a new hire navigate culture and logistics.
- **HRIS:** greytHR portal at https://sailssoftware.greythr.com/.
- **SPOC:** Single Point of Contact for leave coordination.
- **ICC:** Internal Committee under the POSH policy.
- **SMART Goals:** Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound goals.
- **Performance Cycle:** June to May.
- **UAN:** Universal Account Number for Provident Fund.

---

## 4. Welcome and Company Values

Sails' core values, unchanged over the years:
- **Trust in every relationship.**
- **Dedication towards every client's success.**
- **Work only on problems that make the world a better place.**

Sails began as a group of seven "self-driven individuals who work hard and play hard," and seeks colleagues who are driven and motivated even when the boss is not around, obsessed with quality, and free of a "care free" attitude. The environment is meant to be fun, respectful, and fair — judged on performance alone — with high standards, the right to question the quality of others' work, and a sense of urgency to make clients succeed.

## 6. Probation Period

- The first **6 months** are a **probationary period** for all employees, assessing skills, delivery, efficiency, and cultural alignment.
- The Company may **terminate during probation** if not satisfied with effort or performance.
- Probation may be **extended by up to 3 additional months** at the Company's discretion.
- Probationers accrue leave monthly but **cannot take the optional holiday** and are **not eligible for L&D reimbursement** (which needs 1 year).
- Employer-initiated separation during probation gives **15 days** for handover.

## 7. Pre-Start Checklist (Before Day 1)

- [ ] HR sends welcome communication with start date, time, and reporting location.
- [ ] IT prepares **laptop + mouse** (and **monitor** if available per need).
- [ ] Accounts requested: email, Microsoft Teams, **HRIS (greytHR)**, project tools.
- [ ] **ID card** requested/issued.
- [ ] Reporting manager assigns an **onboarding buddy**.
- [ ] New hire added to team calendar, recurring meetings, and **Teams channels** (including **# Employee Leave Notifications**).
- [ ] Document checklist prepared for payroll/PF/tax (bank/ICICI account, **UAN/PF**, IT declaration).

## 11. Company Equipment

- You receive a **laptop and mouse**, configured for productive work; an **external monitor** based on need and availability.
- You are responsible for the **safekeeping** of equipment and peripherals; **lost unattended items** are charged to you.
- **Mishandling** damage (e.g., holding a laptop by the screen), beyond normal wear and tear, is **deducted from salary** with a bill produced.
- Contact the **IT team** for any network issues and for server access.
- Use the **cloud-connected printer** sparingly (sustainability).
- All equipment must be **returned on/immediately after the last working day** at separation.

---

## 12. ID Card Policy

- Wear your **organisation-issued ID card visibly at all times** on premises.
- **Lost/damaged:** inform IT immediately and apply for a replacement via the forms link; **replacement cost is borne by the employee**.
- **Forgotten:** notify IT and collect a **temporary ID** for the day; repeated incidents may lead to warnings.
- Replacement/temporary requests: **https://forms.office.com/r/cpUNxsgkFe**.

---

## 13. Work Hours and Flexibility

- Work starts at **10:30 AM** — **8 hours** excluding lunch and breaks.
- If you'll be **more than 20 minutes late**, post on the channel your expected log-in and log-out times.
- Stepping away for personal work needs **manager/project-leader approval**.
- Using this flexibility **more than 4 hours in a month** requires applying for **leave**.
- Flexibility never excuses missed **project deadlines and deliverables**.

---

## 14. Timesheets

- All employees must enter **client timesheets**.
- Be **detailed** about tasks worked on each day — this benefits both the employee and the Company.
- Accurate, approved timesheets are also required for **F&F** at separation.

---

## 15. Dress Code

- **Formal Wednesdays:** **Business Formal** is mandatory (e.g., full-sleeve formal shirts and formal trousers/shoes for men; business suits, formal dresses, or Indian formals like simple sarees/salwar kameez for women). No jeans, sneakers, loud colours, or casual wear on Wednesdays.
- **Other days:** **Business Casual** (collared/polo shirts, chinos, jeans, kurtas; formal or athletic shoes — **no sandals/slippers/flip-flops**).
- **Never permitted (any day):** slogan/graphic tees, sleeveless/tank tops, ripped jeans, shorts/Bermudas, tracksuits/athletic wear, flip-flops/slippers, caps (except religious/medical), and sheer/revealing clothing.
- Non-compliance escalates: **verbal warning → HR discussion (documented) → corrective action**.

---

## 16. Professional Work Environment

- **Be professional:** No nicknames; address colleagues by **first name**; respect everyone regardless of experience or gender (a serious offence otherwise).
- Maintain **personal hygiene and grooming** and a **clean work environment**.
- **Cafeteria:** Dispose of leftovers before returning plates; do not leave lunchboxes for housekeeping.
- **Washrooms:** Keep them clean, flush, turn off lights, don't wet floors; avoid listed unhygienic behaviours.

---

## 17. Acceptable Use of IT

- IT facilities are for **business/operational** use; **reasonable personal use** is allowed if it doesn't interfere with work.
- **Prohibited:** unlawful use; harassment/bullying; offensive/indecent material; hacking/malware; unauthorised software installs; sharing others' personal data; commercial/for-profit or political-lobbying use; disrupting the network; profanity/obscenity; unauthorised live voice/video relay; downloading non-work entertainment software.
- Treat all network communications as **private property**; subscriptions to mailing lists/bulletin boards must be **reported and pre-approved**.
- Confidential information (code, documentation, notes) **must not be disclosed** outside the Company; all IP/work products belong to the **Company or its clients**.

---

## 18. Payroll, PF, and Tax Setup

- Salary is paid **on or before the 1st** of the month into your **ICICI salary account**.
- Provide your **UAN/PF number** for PF credit.
- File your **IT declaration** in the HRIS; changes before the **10th** of a month apply next cycle.
- Submit **POI** in **October** or at exit; **Form 16** is issued after the financial year.
- Download **payslips** from the HRIS once payroll is released.
- **Compensation is confidential** — discussing salary with colleagues is strongly discouraged and could lead to termination.

---

## 19. Leave and Holidays Orientation

- **16 days** earned leave/year (**1.33/month**); probationers accrue monthly and cannot use advance leave.
- Apply via **HRIS**, post to **# Employee Leave Notifications**, and inform the **SPOC**.
- Notice guidelines: **3 days** (1-day leave), **5 days** (2-day), **3 weeks** (3–5+ days).
- **10 common holidays + 1 optional** (optional only after probation).
- Full details in the **Leave Policy**.

---

## 20. Benefits Orientation

- **Health insurance:** ₹3,00,000 for employee + spouse + 2 children (free; top-up at own cost; ceases on exit).
- **Gratuity:** after 5 years; **PF:** via UAN; **LTA:** domestic travel tax exemption.
- **L&D:** reimbursed training after 1 year; **comp-off** for billed extra work; **maternity** up to 6 months.
- Perks: **free coffee (5th floor)**, **pantry snacks**, **curry plan** (PG/hostel employees without family).
- Full details in the **Employee Benefits Guide**.

## 21. Performance Management Orientation

- The performance cycle runs **June to May**.
- Set **SMART goals** with your manager; submit for approval.
- **Mid-year review** for progress and course-correction; **self-assessment** at cycle end; **year-end review** yields a rating that drives **salary hikes**.
- **Learning Agility** (L&D engagement) is a key parameter.
- All performance discussions are **confidential**.

## 22. Grievance Redressal and POSH

- **Grievances** (working conditions, conflicts, policy concerns): raise first with your **manager**; if unresolved, contact **HR**.
- **POSH:** Sails maintains a **gender-neutral** zero-tolerance policy against sexual harassment. Report to the **ICC** in writing (handwritten to ICC or email **icc@sailssoftware.com**).

## 23. Visitors Policy

- Receive **personal visitors at the front lobby**; floors/cabins/workstations are **off-limits**; visitors may go to the cafeteria after signing the register and getting a **Visitor ID**.
- Inform the **Operations Team in advance** for external visitors (name, purpose, duration, access).
- **Escort** visitors throughout; ensure they **return badges and sign out**.

## 24. 30/60/90-Day Goals

### First 30 Days
1. Complete setup, policy reading, and compliance basics.
2. Build relationships with team and buddy; understand the project/client context.
3. Deliver a first small task with quality and on time.

### First 60 Days
1. Take ownership of a defined work area with reducing supervision.
2. Enter accurate timesheets consistently and meet team norms.
3. Set/confirm SMART goals aligned to team and organisational objectives.

### First 90 Days
1. Contribute independently to deliverables with a sense of urgency for client success.
2. Demonstrate Sails values and quality standards.
3. Be on track for successful completion of probation.

## 26. Worked Examples

### 26.1 Late Arrival
A new hire will be ~40 minutes late on Day 3. They post expected log-in/log-out times on the channel (required when >20 minutes late).

### 26.2 Forgotten ID
A new hire forgets their ID card; they notify **IT** and collect a **temporary ID** for the day.

### 26.3 First Leave Request
A new hire (post-probation) wants 1 day off — they inform the manager **3 days ahead**, apply on HRIS, and post to the leave channel + SPOC.

### 26.4 Formal Wednesday
A new hire wears jeans and sneakers on a Wednesday — non-compliant; the manager gives a **verbal warning** first.

### 26.5 IT Declaration Timing
A new hire submits their IT declaration on the **8th**; it applies to the **next pay cycle**. Submitting on the 12th would push it a further cycle.

## 27. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. When does my workday start?**
A1. **10:30 AM**, 8 hours excluding lunch/breaks.

**Q2. How long is probation?**
A2. **6 months**, extendable by up to **3 months**.

**Q3. Where do I apply for leave?**
A3. On the **HRIS (greytHR)**, plus post to **# Employee Leave Notifications** and inform the **SPOC**.

**Q4. What do I wear?**
A4. **Business Formal on Wednesdays**, **Business Casual** other days; no slogan tees, shorts, flip-flops, or ripped jeans on any day.

**Q5. What if I forget or lose my ID card?**
A5. Notify **IT**; forgotten → temporary ID; lost/damaged → apply for a replacement (cost borne by you) via the forms link.

**Q6. Can I install my own software on my laptop?**
A6. No — **unauthorised software installation is prohibited** under the Acceptable Use Policy.

**Q7. How do I get paid and see my payslip?**
A7. Salary is deposited to your **ICICI account** on/before the 1st; **payslips** are on the HRIS.

**Q8. Which topics are off-limits at work?**
A8. **Religion, caste, and politics.**

**Q9. Who do I talk to about a problem or grievance?**
A9. Your **manager** first; then **HR**. For harassment, the **ICC** (icc@sailssoftware.com).

**Q10. When can I take the optional holiday or claim L&D?**
A10. The optional holiday is **after probation**; L&D reimbursement needs **1 year** of service.
