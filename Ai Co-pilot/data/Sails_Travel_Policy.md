## 1. Purpose

This policy governs travel at Sails Software Solutions, covering (a) the **Leave Travel Allowance (LTA)** tax-exemption benefit on personal travel taken during leave, and (b) the principles for **business travel** undertaken on the Company's behalf. It helps employees understand eligibility, conditions, documentation, and the tax treatment under the **Income Tax Act, 1961**, while ensuring compliance and cost discipline.

---

## 2. Scope and Applicability

- **LTA** applies to all **salaried employees** who have LTA as part of their salary structure.
- **Business travel** provisions apply to any employee travelling on authorised Company/operational business.
- The Company reserves the right to modify this policy based on changes in taxation rules or organisational needs.

---

## 3. Definitions and Glossary

- **LTA (Leave Travel Allowance):** A salary component granting tax exemption on actual domestic travel taken during leave.
- **Block Period:** A fixed four-year window for counting LTA claims; the current block is **2023–2026**.
- **Eligible Family:** Spouse, children, and **dependent** parents and siblings.
- **Domestic Travel:** Travel **within India**; foreign travel does **not** qualify for LTA.
- **POI:** Proof of Investment, submitted to Finance for tax processing.
- **Financial Year (FY):** 01 April – 31 March; LTA exemption applies in the FY of travel.
- **Recognised Transport:** Air, train, bus, or other recognised mode.
- **Business Travel:** Travel undertaken for Company/operational/client purposes.

---

## 4. Leave Travel Allowance (LTA) — Overview

The LTA policy provides employees with **tax benefits on travel expenses incurred during leave**, under the Income Tax Act, 1961. LTA is **not a cash reimbursement**; it is an **exemption** applied to the eligible travel component of salary when valid travel is undertaken and documented.

---

## 5. LTA Eligibility

- Applies to **all salaried employees** who are eligible for LTA as part of their **salary structure**.
- The employee must actually **be on leave** and must **actually undertake** the travel.

---

## 6. LTA Scope of Exemption

- Exemption covers **actual travel expenses** for the employee and **eligible family members** (spouse, children, dependent parents, and siblings).
- Applies **only to domestic travel within India**. **Foreign travel does not qualify.**
- Travel may be by **air, train, bus, or any other recognised mode** of transport.

---

## 7. LTA Conditions

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Leave Requirement** | Claim only when **on leave** with travel expenses incurred during that period |
| **Mode of Travel** | Only **recognised** transport (air/train/bus, etc.) |
| **Travel with Family** | Spouse, children, parents, siblings — must be **dependent** |
| **Actual Travel** | Travel must **actually** be undertaken; unutilised allowance is not exempt |
| **Block Period** | Claims counted within a **four-year block** (e.g., 2023–2026) |

---

## 8. LTA Frequency and Block Period

- LTA exemption can be claimed **twice within each four-year block period**.
- The current block is **2023–2026**.
- Employees must claim LTA for each trip as per the block.
- If no claim is made in a particular year, it can be **carried forward** within the block, but the **maximum allowable claims remain two per block**.

---

## 9. LTA Taxation and Exemption Amount

- The exemption is limited to the **actual travel expenses incurred** **or** the **amount specified in the salary structure**, **whichever is lower**.
- If LTA is **not availed** in a given year, it is treated as **taxable income**.
- Foreign-travel expenses are **not** exempt.

---

## 10. LTA Documentation and Proof

Employees must submit:
- **Travel Tickets:** Copies of air/train/bus tickets as proof of travel.
- **Leave Records:** Evidence that leave was taken during the travel period.
- **Family Proof:** Relationship/dependency documents (birth certificate, marriage certificate) for spouse, children, parents, or siblings.
- **Other Documents:** Any further documents the Company requests to verify travel.

---

## 11. LTA Claim Process

1. Undertake domestic travel **while on leave**.
2. Submit documentation and travel expenses **within the same financial year**, along with **POI**, to the **Finance Department**.
3. Finance reviews and validates the claim.
4. On validation, the exemption is applied to the employee's salary for the respective financial year.

---

## 12. LTA Carry-Forward

- The exemption applies in the **financial year in which travel occurred**.
- If no claim is made in a year, it can be **carried forward to the next year**, but **total claims per block remain capped at two**.

---

## 13. LTA Special Provisions and Exceptions

- **Non-availability of Leave:** Employees unable to take leave during the block due to exigencies or business needs may be unable to claim the exemption.
- **Group/Official Travel:** If travelling as part of a corporate group or on official business, **LTA does not apply**.
- **Employee Responsibility:** Employees must submit documentation on time and ensure travel is legitimate and during leave.

---

## 14. Business Travel

> The handbook establishes reimbursement mechanics and the LTA exemption; the following business-travel operating norms are Company practice built on those principles and the Acceptable Use / Reimbursement policies.

### 14.1 Authorisation

- All business travel must be **pre-authorised** by the reporting manager and, where costs are material, by Finance.
- The purpose, destination, dates, and estimated cost should be recorded before booking.

### 14.2 Booking and Cost Discipline

- Employees should choose **economical, recognised** transport and accommodation consistent with business needs.
- Travel undertaken on official business is **not** eligible for LTA exemption (Section 13).

### 14.3 Reimbursement of Business Travel

- Business-travel costs incurred on the Company's behalf are reimbursable with **original bills/receipts** (and a **GST bill for purchases above ₹2,000**, quoting **37AAVCS6208A1Z8**), per the Reimbursement Policy.

### 14.4 Conduct While Travelling

- Employees represent Sails and must maintain professional, ethical conduct, data security, and confidentiality at all times.

---

## 15. Visitors and Hosting (Travel to Office)

- All **personal visitors** must be received at the **front office lobby**; floors/cabins/workstations are **off-limits**. Visitors may be taken to the cafeteria after signing the **Visitors Register** and obtaining a **"Visitor" ID card**.
- Before hosting **external visitors**, employees must inform the **Operations Team in advance**, providing the visitor's name, purpose, expected duration, and access requirements.
- Host employees must **escort** visitors throughout the premises and ensure compliance with security procedures.
- On completion, visitors must **return ID badges** to reception and **sign out**.

- **Reimbursement Policy:** Governs business-travel reimbursement mechanics and GST documentation.

## 17. Worked Examples

### 17.1 Simple Domestic LTA
An employee takes 5 days' leave and travels by train within India with their spouse. They submit tickets, leave records, and marriage certificate within the same FY. Exemption = lower of actual fare or LTA salary component.

### 17.2 Exemption Cap
Actual travel cost is ₹40,000; LTA salary component is ₹30,000. Exempt amount = **₹30,000** (the lower of the two).

### 17.3 Foreign Trip
An employee travels abroad on leave. **No LTA exemption** applies; foreign travel does not qualify.

### 17.4 Block Limit
In the 2023–2026 block, an employee has already claimed LTA **twice**. A third claim in the block is **not allowed**.

### 17.5 Carry-Forward
An employee makes no LTA claim in one year. The unused opportunity can be carried forward within the block, but total claims still cannot exceed **two per block**.

### 17.6 Official Travel
An employee attends a client meeting in another city on Company business. This is **business travel** (reimbursable per the Reimbursement Policy) and is **not** LTA-eligible.

### 17.7 Not Availed → Taxable
An employee does not avail LTA in a year and does not carry it forward effectively. The LTA component is treated as **taxable income**.

## 18. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. Is LTA cash I receive?**
A1. No. LTA is a **tax exemption** on actual domestic travel taken during leave, not a cash payout.

**Q2. How often can I claim LTA?**
A2. **Twice within a four-year block** (current block **2023–2026**).

**Q3. Does foreign travel count?**
A3. No. Only **domestic travel within India** qualifies.

**Q4. Who can travel with me under LTA?**
A4. **Spouse, children, and dependent parents and siblings.**

**Q5. What proof do I need?**
A5. **Travel tickets, leave records, and family/dependency proof**, plus any documents Finance requests.

**Q6. When do I submit my LTA claim?**
A6. **Within the same financial year** of travel, with POI, to **Finance**.

**Q7. What if I don't claim LTA in a year?**
A7. It may be **carried forward within the block** (max two claims per block); otherwise it becomes **taxable income**.

**Q8. How is the exempt amount calculated?**
A8. **Lower of** actual travel expense **or** the LTA amount in your salary structure.

**Q9. Can I claim LTA for an official business trip?**
A9. No. **Group/official travel does not qualify** for LTA.

**Q10. Do I have to be on leave to claim LTA?**
A10. Yes. The travel must occur while you are **on leave**, evidenced by leave records.

## 19. Roles and Responsibilities

- **Employee:** Travel domestically while on leave; keep tickets and proofs; submit within the FY; ensure legitimacy.
- **Reporting Manager:** Authorise business travel; verify leave for LTA context.
- **Finance:** Validate LTA claims, apply exemptions, ensure tax compliance, process travel reimbursements.
- **Operations Team:** Manage visitor hosting and premises access.

---

## 20. Compliance and Misuse

- Falsifying travel documentation or claiming exemptions for travel not undertaken is a **serious offence** and may lead to disciplinary action and tax recovery.
- LTA claimed without valid leave records or for foreign/official travel will be **rejected**.
