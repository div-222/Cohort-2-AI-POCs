## 1. Purpose

This policy describes the insurance and protection benefits Sails Software Solutions provides to employees and their families — principally the **group health insurance** cover — and the related employer-funded protections such as **gratuity** and statutory **Provident Fund**. It clarifies coverage, eligibility, the effective period, and what happens on separation, so employees understand the scope and limits of their protection.

---

## 2. Scope and Applicability

- **Group health insurance** applies to employees as provided by the Company, with family coverage as defined in Section 5.
- **Gratuity** applies to all regular employees per the **Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972**.
- **Provident Fund** applies to employees with PF deduction in their salary structure.
- Where statutory law provides a more favourable benefit, the **statute prevails**.

---

## 3. Definitions and Glossary

- **Group Health Insurance:** Company-sponsored medical cover for the employee and defined family.
- **Sum Insured:** The maximum coverage amount under the health policy (**₹3,00,000** base).
- **Top-Up Coverage:** Additional cover an employee may purchase **at their own cost** above the base.
- **Dependent / Family:** For base health cover — **employee + spouse + 2 children**.
- **Gratuity:** A lump-sum statutory benefit for long service, **fully funded by the employer**.
- **Continuous Service:** Unbroken service counted toward gratuity eligibility (**5 years**).
- **PF / UAN:** Provident Fund / Universal Account Number.
- **Separation:** End of employment (resignation, termination, retirement).
- **LWD:** Last Working Day.

---

## 4. Group Health Insurance

- The Company provides **free health insurance coverage of ₹3,00,000** (Sum Insured) to eligible employees.
- The cover is provided at **no cost** to the employee for the base sum insured.
- The benefit is a **group policy** arranged by the Company.

---

## 5. Coverage Composition (Who Is Covered)

The base ₹3,00,000 cover extends to:

| Covered Member | Included in base cover |
|----------------|:----------------------:|
| Employee | Yes |
| Spouse | Yes |
| Child 1 | Yes |
| Child 2 | Yes |
| Additional dependents | Only via top-up at own cost (Section 6) |

> The base structure is **employee + spouse + 2 children** within the single ₹3,00,000 sum insured.

---

## 6. Top-Up / Additional Coverage

- Employees may **opt for extra coverage above ₹3,00,000** at their **own cost**.
- Top-up enrolment, premium, and terms are handled through HR and the insurer per the prevailing offer at renewal.

---

## 7. Effective Period and Cessation

- Health insurance coverage is **effective only during employment** with the Company.
- On **separation**, the cover **ceases** for the employee and the family — it will **no longer cover** them once the employee leaves.
- Employees nearing separation should plan for **alternative/personal cover** to avoid a gap.

---

## 8. Statutory Benefits Linked to Welfare

Beyond the group health policy, employees may be covered by statutory schemes per applicable law and salary structure (for example, **Provident Fund**; and **ESI** where applicable by wage threshold and jurisdiction). These are governed by their respective statutes and the employee's salary structure.

## 9. Gratuity (Employer-Funded Benefit)

Gratuity is a long-service protection, **fully paid by the employer**, under the **Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972**.

### 9.1 Eligibility

- Applies to **all regular employees**.
- Requires completion of **at least five (5) full years of continuous service**.
- **Payable on retirement, resignation, or termination**, subject to completing the required service period.

### 9.2 Calculation

```
Gratuity = (15 × Last Drawn Basic Salary × Completed Years of Service) / 26
```

### 9.3 Tax

- Gratuity payments are **subject to applicable tax laws**; the Company deducts necessary taxes at source per prevailing regulations.

---

## 10. Provident Fund (PF)

- Employees with **PF deduction** in their salary structure must provide their **UAN/PF number** for timely credit to the PF account.
- PF is a statutory retirement-savings benefit governed by EPF regulations.

---

## 11. Claims and Support

- For **health insurance** claims (cashless or reimbursement), contact **HR**, who liaises with the insurer/TPA per the prevailing process.
- Keep medical documents, bills, and policy/ID details ready for any claim.
- For **gratuity** and **PF**, HR and Finance coordinate processing on separation and at the applicable life events.

> Specific insurer, TPA, network hospitals, and claim forms are communicated by HR at enrolment/renewal and may change year to year.

## 12. Interactions with Other Policies

- **Separation / F&F:** Health cover ceases on separation; gratuity (if eligible) and PF are settled around the LWD/F&F.
- **Leave / Maternity:** Maternity benefits are governed by the Leave Policy and applicable statute; medical cover applies during employment.
- **Salary:** PF requires UAN; salary structure determines PF/insurance components.
- **Benefits:** Insurance is one component of the broader Employee Benefits framework.

## 13. Worked Examples

### 13.1 Family Cover
A married employee with two children is covered, along with spouse and both children, within the single **₹3,00,000** sum insured at no cost.

### 13.2 Third Child / Parent Cover
To cover a third child or a parent, the employee must purchase **top-up coverage at their own cost**.

### 13.3 Coverage on Exit
An employee resigns; from the date of separation, the **group health cover ceases** for them and their family.

### 13.4 Gratuity Eligibility
An employee with **4 years 8 months** of service resigns. They have **not** completed 5 years, so they are **not eligible** for gratuity.

### 13.5 Gratuity Calculation
An employee with a last-drawn basic of **₹40,000** and **6 completed years** receives:
`(15 × 40,000 × 6) / 26 ≈ ₹1,38,462` (subject to tax and statutory caps).

### 13.6 PF Credit Delay
An employee who hasn't shared their **UAN** may face delayed PF credit; sharing the UAN resolves this.

## 14. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1. How much health cover do I get, and at what cost?**
A1. **₹3,00,000** sum insured, **free** for the base cover.

**Q2. Who is covered under my health insurance?**
A2. **You + spouse + 2 children** within the ₹3,00,000 base.

**Q3. Can I add more coverage or more dependents?**
A3. Yes — you can opt for **extra coverage above ₹3,00,000 at your own cost**.

**Q4. Does my insurance continue after I leave?**
A4. No. Coverage is **effective only during employment** and **ceases on separation**.

**Q5. When am I eligible for gratuity?**
A5. After **5 full years of continuous service**, payable on retirement, resignation, or termination.

**Q6. How is gratuity calculated?**
A6. `(15 × last drawn basic × completed years) / 26`.

**Q7. Is gratuity taxed?**
A7. Yes, **per applicable tax laws**; tax is deducted at source.

**Q8. What do I need for my PF to be credited?**
A8. Provide your **UAN/PF number** to ensure timely credit.

**Q9. How do I make a health insurance claim?**
A9. Contact **HR**, who coordinates with the insurer/TPA per the current process.

**Q10. Will my family lose cover the day I resign?**
A10. Yes — the group cover ceases on separation, so arrange **personal cover** to avoid a gap.

## 15. Roles and Responsibilities

- **Employee:** Provide accurate dependent details and UAN; understand the cessation-on-exit rule; arrange top-up/personal cover as needed.
- **HR:** Administer enrolment/renewal, communicate insurer/TPA details, support claims, process gratuity/PF coordination.
- **Finance:** Manage gratuity computation and tax deduction; ensure PF credits.
- **Senior Management:** Approve coverage structure and renewals.

---

## 16. Compliance and Misuse

- Misrepresenting dependents or submitting fraudulent claims is a **serious offence** and may lead to disciplinary action and claim rejection.
- Employees must keep dependent and UAN details current to avoid coverage or credit issues.
